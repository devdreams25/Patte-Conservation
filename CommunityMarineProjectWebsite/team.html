Conservation Projects

Who Are Our Rangers?

Our Rangers are the frontline protectors of our coastal and marine environment. They work every day to safeguard our natural resources, support local communities, and help ensure a sustainable future for everyone.

What They Do

  • Patrol coastal and marine areas
  • Monitor fishing activities and marine wildlife
  • Protect important habitats such as reefs and mangroves
  • Work with communities to promote sustainable practices
  • Report environmental issues and support conservation efforts

Why Their Work Matters

Our Rangers play an important role in preserving healthy oceans and ensuring that marine resources remain available for future generations. Their dedication helps maintain food security, protect biodiversity, and support the livelihoods of coastal communities.

Our Commitment

We value the hard work and courage of our Rangers. Their efforts keep our marine areas safe, healthy, and thriving. Chairman

Rangers Engaging with Fishers at Sea

Our Rangers meet with local fishers at sea to discuss LMMA guidelines and promote sustainable fishing practices. These conversations help strengthen cooperation, protect marine resources, and support the long-term livelihood of fishing communities.

Community-Based Approach

Our team represents the collaborative spirit of the three communities - Pate, Shanga Ishakani, and Shanga Rubu. Each member brings unique local knowledge, professional expertise, and deep commitment to marine conservation.

We believe that effective marine conservation must be community-driven, combining traditional ecological knowledge with modern conservation science. Our diverse team ensures that every voice is heard and every community need is addressed.

Our Commitment:

  • Inclusive community participation
  • Transparent decision-making processes
  • Sustainable livelihood development
  • Long-term marine ecosystem protection
